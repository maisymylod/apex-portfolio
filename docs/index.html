I gave an AI $1,000 and told it to think like Leopold Aschenbrenner.

No real broker, no real money. The whole book is one idea: take the Situational Awareness thesis (transformative AI by ~2027, power is the binding constraint) and the fund's actual May 18 13F, size 13 names by conviction, and let an agent run it on autopilot. Call it vibe investing for people who read the footnotes.

Rule 1 Mirror only. No discretionary picks beyond the published thesis and filing.
Rule 2 Mark to market every weekday. Fully reproducible. Nothing hand-tuned after the fact.

Here's what building it honestly actually taught me, in order:

  1. Absolute P&L is a vanity metric. "Up 2%" means nothing until you put SPY on the same axis. So the agent now computes active return, beta, alpha, tracking error and information ratio every run. Most of the "performance" evaporates the moment there's a benchmark next to it.
  2. Every Sharpe you see on a young book is simulated. The slick 10,000-path Monte Carlo number is a model, not a track record. It's now labelled SIMULATED everywhere so I don't lie to myself later.
  3. The part I can't stop thinking about happened before any of that. A second pass audited the first build — and caught it inventing returns by accident.
DISCOVERY.md — Gap G : silent data-quality failure RESOLVED

When a single ticker's price failed to download, the original pipeline didn't error. It quietly dropped that position from the total — its market value just vanished — and wrote the resulting fake drop into the permanent history file as a real loss. Phantom P&L, invented by accident, baked into the record forever.

The builder didn't "know" it was wrong. The audit pass (now in DISCOVERY.md, Gap G) caught it, and the next version shipped a data-quality guard: a missing price marks the day degraded instead of silently rewriting the book. Same audit found the learning loop's docstring describing SPY-relative math the code never actually did.

The agent cheated. The audit caught it. Neither of them knew anything was wrong.

Three questions I don't have answers to:

  • Is intelligence even the bottleneck in markets, or does the market just eat it for breakfast?
  • If a pipeline can invent fake P&L by accident, how much of the "AI trading" out there is exactly one day psychic too?
  • My agent needed an auditor to stay honest. Who audits yours?
